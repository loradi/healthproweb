Medicine

Your child's practical medical history should also include any medications, with their respective doses, that your child takes on a regular basis. Some medications interact with others, that is, they react inappropriately when combined with each other. Therefore, health personnel will need this information before administering any medication to your child. You should know when your child took the medication for the last time and in what amount.

Alergies

This is especially important if your child is allergic to any medication, such as penicillin or other antibiotics. Food allergies, dyes or contrast materials (substances used in tests such as CT-axial computed tomography) are also important; That is, include anything your child has had an allergic reaction to in the past. Children who have been hospitalized may have developed a latex allergy.
